Our Impact

Oonchiumpa's culturally responsive programmes create meaningful change in the lives of Aboriginal youth and their communities. Our evidence-based approach ensures we can demonstrate the effectiveness of our interventions while staying true to cultural values and community needs.

Holistic Support Model

Our approach addresses the complex needs of Aboriginal youth through:

Education & Employment

Supporting youth to re-engage with education and develop pathways to meaningful employment

Health & Wellbeing

Improving access to health services and supporting mental, emotional, and physical wellbeing

Housing & Basic Needs

Ensuring youth have safe accommodation and their essential needs are met

Cultural Connection

Strengthening identity and connection to culture, country, and community

Programme Highlights

Cultural Connection

▼

The project successfully strengthened participants' cultural identity through participation in culturally connected activities, including on-country trips and yarning circles.

  • Increased cultural knowledge and connection to heritage
  • Active engagement in culturally based activities
  • Empowerment through cultural authority and leadership
Impact Highlight

Participants developed meaningful relationships with family and community, enhancing their cultural identity and reducing anti-social behaviours.

Personal Development

▼

Participants demonstrated significant growth in confidence and resilience, with many now having the life skills to enable them to participate in community programs and events.

  • Increased independence and confidence
  • Ability to articulate needs and advocate for themselves
  • Engagement in life skills related tasks
Impact Highlight

Young people are now able to budget, shop, and manage personal tasks independently, contributing to their personal growth and leadership skills.

Educational and Career Outcomes

▼

Improved school attendance and academic engagement were noted, along with increased interest in pursuing employment opportunities.

  • 72% of participants returned to school or alternative education pathways
  • Increased motivation and participation in educational activities
  • Interest in career development and employment
Impact Highlight

Participants expressed a desire to continue their education and pursue career opportunities, supported by tailored guidance and skill-building initiatives.

Our Approach to Evaluation

We use a mixed-methods approach to evaluate our programmes and demonstrate their effectiveness:

Quantitative Data

We collect and analyse data on attendance, engagement, referrals, and outcomes to measure programme effectiveness.

Qualitative Feedback

We gather stories, testimonials, and feedback from participants, families, and community members.

Community Consultation

We regularly consult with community leaders and stakeholders to ensure our programmes meet community needs.

Continuous Improvement

We use evaluation findings to refine and improve our programmes and approaches.

Strategic Framework: Three Horizons

Our strategic vision employs the Three Horizons model to address immediate needs while building toward transformative, long-term system change in Aboriginal youth services.

Horizon 1: Immediate (0-2 Years)

Strengthening foundations with practical, achievable steps using current capabilities.

  • 1 Cultivate stronger cultural connections for youth
  • 2 Enhance school engagement and attendance
  • 3 Secure safe sleeping arrangements
  • 4 Expand mentoring and role model access

Horizon 2: Transitional (2-5 Years)

Expanding initiatives, developing infrastructure and deepening collaborations.

  • 1 Establish Youth Cultural Centre in Alice Springs
  • 2 Double programme reach to over 40 youth
  • 3 Launch indigenous tourism/cultural enterprise
  • 4 Create Youth Leadership Council

Horizon 3: Transformational (5-20+ Years)

Realising Arrernte self-determination in youth services and enterprise.

  • 1 Achieve economic self-determination through enterprise
  • 2 Transform youth justice system with cultural approaches
  • 3 Close the gap in education and employment outcomes
  • 4 Establish sustainable funding independent of government

Priority Investment Areas

Safe Sleeping Facility

Establish a youth residential facility to provide stable accommodation and improve access to education and services.

Youth Cultural Centre

Create a dedicated space for cultural learning, mentoring, and programme activities as a hub for coordination.

Cultural Enterprise Incubator

Develop infrastructure and training for Aboriginal tourism and cultural enterprises led by young people.
