## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### The Fat Tyape Food Truck/Cafe - Youth Microenterprise

**One-Page Summary for Funders**

---

**Organisation:** Oonchiumpa Consultancy and Services
**Location:** Mbantua (Alice Springs), Northern Territory
**Project:** The Fat Tyape Food Truck/Cafe - Youth Microenterprise
**Total Funding Request:** $1,485,000 over 3 years

---

### The Opportunity

Aboriginal youth in Central Australia face significant barriers to education and employment. Many are disconnected from mainstream pathways, leading to increased justice system contact and poorer life outcomes. There is currently **no youth-led food enterprise or after-hours nutrition service** for young people in Mbantua.

### The Solution

"The Fat Tyape" (Arrernte: witchetty grub) is an Aboriginal-led youth microenterprise that provides:

- **Hospitality training and paid employment** for disengaged Aboriginal youth
- **Accredited certification pathways** (Food Safety, Hospitality Certificate II/III)
- **After-hours nutrition drop-in service** during peak risk hours (6pm-10pm)
- **Community catering** that builds positive visibility for Aboriginal young people

### Why Oonchiumpa?

Oonchiumpa has a **proven track record** of transforming outcomes for Aboriginal youth:

| Outcome | Result |
|---------|--------|
| Operation Luna reduction | 95% (18 of 19 youth removed from watch list) |
| School re-engagement | 72% of participants |
| Program retention | 89% |
| Service connections | 71 young people referred to support services |

### Alignment to Government Priorities

| Priority Framework | Alignment |
|--------------------|-----------|
| **Closing the Gap Target 7** | Youth employment and education (67% target by 2031) |
| **Closing the Gap Target 11** | Reduced youth incarceration (30% reduction by 2031) |
| **NIAA Food Security** | $71.4M commitment in 2025 Implementation Plan |
| **Mparntwe Youth Action Plan 2023-2027** | Young people living "positive and healthy lives" |
| **Justice Reinvestment** | $69M National Program in Alice Springs |

### Key Risk Mitigation Details

#### Food Safety
- All staff and trainees complete Food Safety Certificate before handling food
- Daily temperature logs and cleaning checklists
- Regular health inspections welcomed
- Product liability insurance maintained
- Clear incident response procedures

#### Youth Engagement
- Flexible entry points (not one cohort start date)
- Intensive support for first 4 weeks
- Peer support from experienced trainees
- Connection to Oonchiumpa's broader services
- Family engagement to support participation

#### Financial Management
- Monthly financial reporting
- Quarterly board review
- Annual independent audit
- Separate bank account for enterprise
- Clear delegations and approvals
