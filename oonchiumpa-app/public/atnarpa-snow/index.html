Continuity

From Pastoralist to Tourism and On-Country Learning

Our ancestors shared knowledge through pastoral life at Loves Creek, including station duties, stock work, and family care under difficult conditions. Today we continue that legacy through cultural tourism, on-Country educational training, and intergenerational learning.

Land Rights

Land Claim and Present-Day Stewardship

Loves Creek Station was one of the earliest pastoral leases in Central Australia. Land claim recognition enables us to lead our own future at Atnarpa and deliver restoration, activation, and long-term planning under Traditional Owner leadership.

Today, we continue sharing culture with care and invite respectful participation guided by our rules and values.

Main Build Priority

Main Build at Campground

Our immediate infrastructure focus is the campground conference precinct, centered between the dongas, with supporting links to accommodation, amenities, and cultural gathering space. Homestead-side works remain part of the plan as smaller supporting upgrades staged around this core build.

Now: Conference core, deck links, yarning circle, essential amenities. Later: Expanded landscape treatment and homestead-side refinement. Indicative: Approximately 1-2 weeks, crew of five, subject to approvals/logistics/funding.

Conference Core Concept

Two 40-foot containers with a roof between can form a fully enclosed and air-conditioned function/training room. The proposed internal footprint is approximately 11m by 6m (66 sqm).

From this core, open-air decking can connect to accommodation blocks, kitchen access, and a central firepit/yarning area to support workshops, conferences, and community gatherings.

Material selection, footings, approvals, and transport to site are treated as key delivery constraints in the staging and quote pathway.

Proposed Infrastructure Development Points

This list translates the sketches into practical campground upgrade points, so scope is clear before approvals, procurement, and site mobilization.

  1. Conference Structure: Two 40-foot containers joined with a roof between, fully enclosed and air-conditioned (approximately 11m x 6m / 66 sqm) for functions, conferences, training, and gatherings.
  2. Decking and Circulation: Open-air decking joins the central structure to accommodation and kitchen access, creating one connected communal movement path.
  3. Accommodation Upgrades: Existing dongas are upgraded into three accommodation blocks linked to the conference structure via decking.
  4. Fire Pit and Gathering: A central fire pit / yarning point anchors cultural gathering and story-sharing in the campground heart.
  5. Homestead Supporting Upgrades: Two toilet and shower blocks at the homestead are installed/upgraded through staged reconfiguration of existing infrastructure.

10-Year Pathway

10-Year Vision: Generational Wealth, Enterprise, and Country (2026-2036)

Our long-term direction is to build intergenerational wealth through family-led enterprise on Country at Atnarpa, while protecting cultural authority and community wellbeing. We are carrying this work forward from the station lease history, including the Hewett family lease period, into a future led by Traditional Owners.

This pathway links infrastructure, programs, training, and governance so young people can move into practical employment, business leadership, and long-term stewardship of land and culture.

Phase 1 - 2026 to 2028

Foundation and Enterprise Setup

Complete design, approvals, and enabling works while setting up the first family-led enterprise operations. Start early youth pathways, on-Country learning delivery, and governance systems that support staged growth.

Phase 2 - 2029 to 2031

Campground Precinct Activation

Deliver and activate the campground conference precinct, connected circulation, and essential service edge so education, events, cultural tourism, and training can operate as one family-led system.

Phase 3 - 2032 to 2036

Intergenerational Transfer and Scale

Scale revenue streams, strengthen enterprise capability, and transfer leadership to the next generation through mentoring, ownership pathways, and staged homestead upgrades that support long-term stability.

Outcomes

Measurement Framework

Participation

Track how many families and young people from Alice Springs and surrounding homelands engage in Oonchiumpa-led workshops, cultural programs, tourism activities, and repeat visits on Country.

Pathways

Track progression from first participation into training, paid work, mentoring, and enterprise roles so the Bloomfield children and regional youth build leadership, confidence, and practical skills on Country.

Delivery

Track staged delivery of campground and homestead works, operational readiness, and transition planning through the current Hewett lease period so the next 10 years strengthen Indigenous wealth and create a family-led case study for the NT and Australia.

Staging Notes

Practical Dependencies

Key dependencies include container transport, footing design, engineering checks, service trenching, and compliance approvals for enclosed and air-conditioned operations.

The build can scale up or down by package, while preserving the campground conference core as the primary infrastructure priority.
