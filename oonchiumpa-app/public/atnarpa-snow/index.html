Atnarpa Proposal 2026-2036

Building Family Wealth and Cultural Continuity on Country

Traditional Owners of Loves Creek Station and Oonchiumpa Consultancy and Services

Supported by A Curious Tractor. A ten-year pathway for intergenerational opportunity, cultural leadership, and community-led enterprise in and around Alice Springs.

Acknowledgement of Country

We acknowledge the Traditional Owners of Atnarpa and Loves Creek Station, and we pay our respects to Elders past and present. This proposal is our commitment to care for Country and strengthen culture, community, and family through our own leadership.

Legacy

Building on Our Family Legacy

Our family legacy carries responsibility and pride. The homestead and surrounding Country hold both the weight of history and the strength of survival.

"We know what we wanna do on our land, and we know how to get there." - Kristy Bloomfield

We continue this work through cultural authority, practical delivery, and shared commitment to future generations.

Continuity

From Pastoralist to Tourism and On-Country Learning

Our ancestors shared knowledge through pastoral life, station work, and family teaching. That practice continues through cultural tourism, educational immersion, and intergenerational transfer of knowledge.

Land Rights

Land Claim and Present-Day Stewardship

Land rights recognition enables us to lead our own future at Atnarpa. It allows restoration, activation, and long-term planning under Traditional Owner leadership.

Today, we continue sharing culture with care and invite respectful participation guided by our rules and values.

"Our kids 10 years time, that's our focus at the moment." - Kylie Bloomfield

10-Year Pathway

Delivery Approach and Phasing (2026-2036)

Phase 1 - 2026 to 2028

Design and Enabling Works

Complete design, approvals, and site-enabling works while launching early cultural and youth programs.

Phase 2 - 2029 to 2031

Core Build and Launch

Deliver the function space, covered connections, and essential services. Open for education, events, and tourism activity.

Phase 3 - 2032 to 2036

Growth and Refinement

Scale programs, refine operations, and transfer leadership pathways across family and community.

Program Pillars

Community-Centered Activities

On-Country Workshops

Cultural learning, storytelling, and identity-strengthening led by Traditional Owners and mentors.

Youth Training Pathways

Skill development for employment in hospitality, land care, logistics, and cultural program delivery.

Community Events

Shared gatherings, intergenerational exchange, and respectful visitor engagement on Country.

Cultural Enterprise

Story sharing and locally led offerings that build family income and protect cultural authority.

Closing Invitation

Partnership That Respects Community Leadership

Atnarpa is a place of memory and possibility. We are seeking partnership that strengthens our capacity to deliver our own vision over the next ten years.

We invite Snow Foundation to walk with us as we build family wealth, cultural continuity, and opportunity for young people and the wider Alice Springs community.
