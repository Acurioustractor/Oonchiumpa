# Oonchiumpa Impact Measurement Framework

**Date**: October 26, 2025
**Version**: 1.0
**Purpose**: Comprehensive impact measurement system for all Oonchiumpa services

---

## 🎯 Executive Summary

This framework provides a culturally appropriate, evidence-based approach to measuring and demonstrating the impact of Oonchiumpa's community programs across Central Australia. It integrates:

- **Theory of Change** methodology
- **Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander-specific** outcomes frameworks
- **Community-led evaluation** principles
- **Multi-dimensional impact** measurement
- **Cultural healing** indicators

---

## 📚 Framework Foundation

### Research-Based Models

Based on comprehensive research of Australian and international best practices for Indigenous program evaluation:

1. **Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health Performance Framework** (AIHW 2025)
2. **Closing the Gap National Agreement** targets and outcomes
3. **Indigenous Advancement Strategy Evaluation Framework** (NIAA)
4. **The Healing Foundation's Theory of Change**
5. **Community-led impact assessment** models

### Core Principles

✅ **Community Control**: Oonchiumpa designs, delivers, and measures its own programs
✅ **Cultural Safety**: Evaluation methods respect cultural protocols and sovereignty
✅ **Holistic Wellbeing**: Measures physical, emotional, social, spiritual, and cultural health
✅ **Strengths-Based**: Focuses on community assets, not deficits
✅ **Long-Term Impact**: Tracks short, medium, and long-term outcomes
✅ **Data Sovereignty**: Oonchiumpa owns and controls all program data

## 📊 Impact Measurement Model

### Theory of Change Structure

```
INPUTS → ACTIVITIES → OUTPUTS → OUTCOMES → IMPACT
```

#### Level 1: INPUTS
**What we invest**:
- Funding (NIAA, partnerships)
- Staff (cultural brokers, mentors, elders)
- Infrastructure (Atnarpa Homestead, vehicles, equipment)
- Cultural knowledge and authority
- Community relationships (32+ partners)

#### Level 2: ACTIVITIES
**What we do**:
- Youth mentorship sessions
- On-country cultural camps
- Deep listening circles
- Service navigation
- Community events
- Cultural healing programs
- Skill-building workshops

#### Level 3: OUTPUTS
**Direct deliverables** (What we produce):
- Number of participants
- Sessions delivered
- Hours of cultural programming
- Partnerships activated
- Events held
- Stories collected

#### Level 4: OUTCOMES
**Changes for participants** (Short, Medium, Long-term):

**Short-term (0-6 months)**:
- Increased cultural connection
- Improved school attendance
- Enhanced self-esteem and identity
- New skills acquired
- Stronger peer support

**Medium-term (6-18 months)**:
- Cultural identity strengthened
- School re-engagement sustained
- Leadership skills developed
- Community connections deepened
- Reduced justice involvement

**Long-term (18+ months)**:
- Intergenerational healing
- Community capacity increased
- Cultural knowledge transmitted
- Sustainable behavior change
- Systemic impact (partnerships, policy)

#### Level 5: IMPACT
**Lasting community change**:
- Cultural revitalization
- Intergenerational wellbeing
- Self-determination strengthened
- Community resilience enhanced
- Cultural sovereignty exercised

## 🗂️ Data Collection Methods

### Quantitative Data

**Participation Metrics**:
- Attendance registers
- Enrollment records
- Demographic data (age, gender, language group, community)
- Session logs (date, duration, activities, participants)

**Stories and Narratives**:

**Visual Documentation**:
- Photos from programs and events (with consent)
- Videos showcasing activities and outcomes
- Art and creative expressions
- Cultural artifacts and documentation

**Assessments**:
- Self-assessment surveys (cultural connection, wellbeing, skills)
- Peer assessments (leadership, collaboration)
- Elder and cultural authority evaluations
- Partner satisfaction surveys

## 🔗 Linking Data Across Systems

### Integration Points

**Documents → Outcomes**:
- Reports contain outcome data → Extract and categorize
- Good news stories → Link to specific outcomes
- Evaluations → Map to Theory of Change

**Stories → Impact**:
- Interview transcripts → Identify themes and outcomes
- Video testimonials → Tag with relevant indicators
- Photo documentation → Associate with activities and outcomes

**Services → Results**:
- Youth Mentorship → Track school re-engagement, cultural connection
- Atnarpa Camps → Measure cultural knowledge, wellbeing, connection to country
- True Justice → Monitor justice outcomes, healing progress, community safety
- Cultural Brokerage → Count successful referrals, service access, community satisfaction

### Data Linkage Schema

```
DOCUMENT
  ├─ contains → OUTCOMES (extracted via AI)
  ├─ evidences → SERVICE IMPACTS
  ├─ references → PARTICIPANTS (anonymized)
  ├─ supports → GRANT APPLICATIONS
  └─ demonstrates → THEORY OF CHANGE

STORY/VIDEO
  ├─ illustrates → INDIVIDUAL OUTCOMES
  ├─ showcases → CULTURAL ACTIVITIES
  ├─ highlights → COMMUNITY IMPACT
  ├─ provides → QUALITATIVE EVIDENCE
  └─ connects to → SPECIFIC SERVICES

SERVICE
  ├─ delivers → ACTIVITIES
  ├─ produces → OUTPUTS
  ├─ achieves → OUTCOMES
  ├─ creates → IMPACT
  ├─ documented by → DOCUMENTS, STORIES, MEDIA
  └─ measured by → KPIS

PARTICIPANT (Anonymized)
  ├─ engages in → SERVICES
  ├─ experiences → OUTCOMES
  ├─ contributes to → STORIES (with consent)
  ├─ demonstrates → IMPACT
  └─ measured by → INDICATORS
```

## 📝 Data Collection Templates

### Template 1: Activity Log
**Purpose**: Track all program activities

**Fields**:
- Date and time
- Service area (Youth Mentorship, Atnarpa, True Justice, Cultural Brokerage)
- Activity type (mentoring session, on-country camp, deep listening, referral)
- Participants (count, age range, gender, language group)
- Duration
- Location
- Facilitators/staff
- Cultural elements included
- Outcomes observed
- Notes/reflections

### Template 2: Participant Outcome Tracker
**Purpose**: Monitor individual progress (anonymized aggregate data)

**Fields**:
- Unique ID (non-identifying)
- Baseline assessment (cultural connection, wellbeing, skills)
- Service engagement (frequency, duration, completion)
- Milestones achieved
- Outcome indicators (school attendance, cultural activities, leadership roles)
- Follow-up assessments (3, 6, 12 months)
- Qualitative feedback
- Story contribution (with consent)

### Template 3: Partnership Impact Record
**Purpose**: Document collaboration outcomes

**Fields**:
- Partner organization name and type
- Relationship start date
- Collaboration activities
- Referrals made (count, success rate)
- Joint programs delivered
- Resources shared
- Outcomes achieved together
- Challenges and learnings
- Satisfaction rating
- Cultural safety assessment

### Template 4: Cultural Indicator Assessment
**Purpose**: Measure cultural outcomes

**Fields**:
- Service/activity
- Cultural protocols followed (Elder guidance, language use, country connection)
- Traditional knowledge transmitted (topics, methods, hours)
- Cultural authority recognized (decision-making, leadership)
- Intergenerational connection (elders + youth engaged)
- Cultural confidence (self-assessment)
- Identity strengthening (qualitative feedback)
- Community cultural activities (participation rates)

### Template 5: Story & Media Documentation
**Purpose**: Link stories to outcomes and services

**Fields**:
- Story ID and title
- Format (written, audio, video, photo)
- Date collected
- Consent level (public, community, private, sacred)
- Service area illustrated
- Outcomes demonstrated
- Themes and tags
- Participant involvement (anonymized)
- Cultural sensitivity level
- Elder review status
- Usage permissions
